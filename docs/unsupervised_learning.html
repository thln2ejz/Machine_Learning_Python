Self-Organizing Maps¶

SOMs are iterative methods that construct a topographic mapping from a high dimensional feature space (data space) to a lower dimensional map space which is two dimensional in most cases. This latter space consists of a net or grid of cluster centers each of which has a corresponding position in the original data space. Cluster centers that are neighbors in data space are also neighbors in map space. As the self-organizing map is trained, the centers in data space move around as to cover the data in an optimal manner. One may then view map space in order to gain insight on the structure of the data in the original data space.

from minisom import MiniSom 
from sklearn import datasets

iris = datasets.load_iris()
X = iris.data    #ndarray     

n_neurons = 10 
m_neurons = 10
X = X[:,-2:]
som = MiniSom(n_neurons, m_neurons, X.shape[1], sigma=1.5, learning_rate=0.05, neighborhood_function='gaussian')

som.pca_weights_init(X)
som.train(X, 5000, verbose=False)  

plt.imshow(som.distance_map().T,cmap='hot') #heatmap
